General information

The aeromodeling is one kind of sports, based on the constructing scaled or self-developed mini-aircrafts.
In some cases models can be made in middle, big and true scale
In aeromodeling, the aircrafts can be clasified by some categories:

  • Types
  • Kinds
  • Classes
Types of aeromodels

In this paragraph we we'll talk about types of avia-models
In aeromodeling, there're several types of them:

  • Gliders
  • Planes
  • Jets
  • Drones

Let's check them out in details

Gliders

This type of models is very polpular for training of pilotage, especially for new pilots.
This type of models is also requered for the settings of the speed, duratation, hight and other types of gliding records.
Also there's one special kind of gliders- motogliders.
Those gliders are equipped by motor, that can be electric or fuel-motor.
In nowdays, this type of gliders has the most number of highest records in duratetion, hight and distance of gliding.

Planes

This is the most popular class of avia-models, due to installation of engines or motors.
And they have different kinds, that can be set in categories.
We'll talk about them below.

Jets

This type of models is also can be set as planes, but...
This kind of planes is so difficult, fast and heavy, that can't be set in any class of FAI.
You can also read more information about Jet models here.

Drones

Drones are the kinds of the multirotor R/C models, that equipped with the autopilot and FPV-camera.
They can be used in different ways: from the simple flights and racing, until the aerial recording and importaint researches.
They are also callen the UAVs.

History of aeromodeling

The history of aviation, generally, began in Ancient Greece, while the mith of Daedalos and Ikarus was created.
But there's another misterious fact, that the ancient scientist Archytas, maybe, has developed something was looking like the aircraft, the 'peristerion'
But we must not forget, that these are just myths.
In fact, the history of real aircrafts begins in Medievial Europe, in 850's, when the Abbas ibn Firnas made a jump in Cordoba, Spain, reportedly covering his body with vulture feathers and attaching two wings to his arms. But this jump wasn't successful due to the primitivity of construction.
The one more step to the sky were the Chinese kites, that was able to carry humans on some hight.
And, finally, the giant leaps forward were the flights of Otto Lilienthal and Wright brothers.
After the flight of Wright brothers, the FAI was founded on 14 October 1905, and is headquartered in Lausanne, Switzerland.

And since 1910-1920, the era of aeromodeling begins.
First, the aeromodels were very simple. Before and few years after WWs there were gliders and control line planes.
But in 1950's and 1960's aeromodelers began to use the radio controlled models.
And those models were very expencive and difficult in service.
In nowdays, the aeromodels became so suffisticated, that can be used in many everyday life's aspects by everyone.
